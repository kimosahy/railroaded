# Adventure 01: The Ruins of Bernice

*A starter dungeon for 4 Level 1 characters, heavily inspired by Lineage 2's Ruins of Agony, Elven Ruins, School of Dark Arts, and the lore of Aden.*

---

## Setting: The World of Aden (Adapted)

Long ago, the Giants ruled. Created by the twin gods Einhasad and Gran Kain, they were lords of all living things. The gods' children — Shilen, Paagrio, Maphr, Sayha — each created a race to serve: Elves from water, Orcs from fire, Dwarves from earth. Humans? Humans were made from leftovers. The garbage. The stagnant water, the dying fire, the barren dirt.

And yet — somehow — humans survived everything. The Giants fell. The gods went silent. The kingdom of Aden rose from the rubble, ruled by the young King Raoul, held together by fragile alliances and old grudges.

In the south, near the town of Gludio, the farming villages that once fed the kingdom lie in ruin. The wars of Beleth the demon lord and the Gracian invasion consumed them. Now only the undead wander what's left.

**Bernice** was the largest of these villages. Prosperous once. Gone now. But something stirs beneath it — in tunnels the villagers dug as shelters during the wars, tunnels that go deeper than anyone remembers building.

---

## The Hook: The Tavern at Gludio

The party meets at **The Broken Millstone**, a tavern in Gludio that smells like wet stone and burnt grain. The bartender, a one-armed ex-soldier named **Harmon**, has a job board. One posting stands out:

> *BERNICE RUINS — Undead activity increasing. Merchants reporting attacks on the south road. The Magistrate's guard won't go near it. 200 adena reward for clearing the upper chambers. Additional bounty for information about what's causing the resurgence.*
> *— Posted by Trader Unoren, South Gate*

**Why each character cares:**
- **Brog:** People on the south road are getting hurt. That's enough.
- **Wren:** 200 adena plus whatever isn't nailed down in a ruin nobody's looted properly.
- **Sylith:** The ruins predate the current settlement. Pre-war tunnels suggest older construction — possibly Giant-era artifacts in the deep levels. The undead resurgence implies a magical catalyst worth investigating.
- **Dolgrim:** Tunnels that go "deeper than anyone remembers building" means someone built them with intent. Dwarven engineering or not, he wants to see the construction. Also, the structural instability is a professional insult.

**Trader Unoren** (quest giver) can be found at the South Gate. He's a nervous, twitchy merchant who lost two caravans to undead attacks in the last month. He'll explain:
- The ruins have always had some undead — old war dead, cursed ground, standard Aden problems
- But recently they've been *organized*. Moving in groups. Patrolling.
- Something in the deep tunnels is commanding them
- He wants bone fragments and skulls as proof of clearing (and to sell to the Ivory Tower researchers, though he won't admit that)

## Dungeon Map: The Ruins of Bernice

### Overworld: The Village Ruins

Open air. Collapsed stone buildings, overgrown fields, a dry well in the center. Faded merchant signs still hang from rusted brackets. The wind makes them creak. It sounds like whispers if you're not paying attention. It sounds like whispers if you ARE paying attention too.

**Enemies (patrol):** 2-3 **Skeleton Bowmen** wandering the perimeter. Ranged, weak, but they'll alert deeper enemies if not handled quietly.

*DM Note: This is the tutorial. Let Wren scout, let Sylith observe their patrol pattern, let Brog just walk in and hit one. Dolgrim will comment on the stonework.*

### Floor 1: The Shelter Tunnels

Accessed through a collapsed cellar behind the old granary. Stone stairs descending into hand-dug tunnels — originally civilian shelters from the Beleth Wars. The walls are packed earth reinforced with timber (Dolgrim will immediately note the timber is load-bearing and rotting — the whole level is a cave-in waiting to happen).

**Room 1A — The Common Shelter**
A large underground chamber where villagers once huddled. Overturned cots, scattered personal belongings covered in dust and cobwebs. A child's wooden toy sits on a shelf, untouched by decay. The air is cold and stale.

*Loot:* A locked strongbox under a collapsed bed frame (DC 12 Thieves' Tools for Wren) — contains 30 adena and a **Faded Letter** from a villager to his wife in Gludin, never sent. Not valuable. But Brog will want to keep it.

**Enemies:** 4 **Misery Skeletons** — reanimated war dead. They rise from the cots when the party enters. Slow, fragile, but unsettling. Their armor is Gludio militia issue — these were the defenders.

**Room 1B — The Collapsed Passage**
A cave-in blocks the main tunnel forward. Rubble, timber, dust.

*Skill challenge:*
- Dolgrim (DC 10 Intelligence/Investigation): identifies which beams are load-bearing. Removing the wrong rubble collapses the ceiling.
- Brog (DC 12 Strength): clears the safe rubble once Dolgrim marks it
- Alternative: Wren finds a narrow side passage (DC 13 Perception) — too small for Brog in plate armor. He'd have to remove it. He won't want to.

**Room 1C — The War Dead Armory**
A small chamber the militia used to store weapons. Mostly rusted and useless. But on a weapon rack in the back:

*Loot:*
- **Intact Gludio Short Sword** — nothing special mechanically, but well-forged. Dolgrim recognizes the maker's mark as Dwarven-apprentice work. Decent craftsmanship. He'll be reluctantly impressed.
- **3 Bundles of Crossbow Bolts** (15 total)
- A **Shield with the Gludio Crest** — functional. If Brog takes it, he finally has something painted on his shield. He didn't choose it. It chose him.

### Floor 2: The Old Tunnels

Below the shelter level, the construction changes. The timber gives way to cut stone. The walls are older — much older. Dolgrim will go quiet here, which is how you know he's impressed. These aren't civilian shelters. This is pre-war construction. Possibly pre-Kingdom.

The air gets colder. There's a faint smell of ozone — magical residue.

**Room 2A — The Dark Corridor**
Long hallway, no light sources. Sylith's darkvision makes her invaluable here. Runes carved into the walls at regular intervals — old Elven script, defaced. Someone chiseled over them with cruder symbols.

*Skill challenge:*
- Sylith (DC 14 Arcana): The original runes were Elven wards — protective magic. The defacing symbols are Dark Elven. Someone systematically disabled the protections. This was deliberate.
- If Sylith reads the Dark Elven symbols (she can — she won't explain how): they're invocations to **Shilen, Goddess of Death**. Prayer, not spellcraft. Devotional.

**Enemies:** 3 **Skeleton Hunters** and 1 **Skeleton Hunter Archer**. These are better equipped than the floor above — actual combat gear, coordinated. The archer hangs back. The hunters try to flank. First sign of intelligent tactics.

**Room 2B — The Devotion Chamber**
A small chapel-like room. Smashed Einhasad altar at the center, replaced with a crude obsidian slab. Candles that burn with pale blue flame — no wax, no wick, just fire that sits on stone. Offerings arranged neatly: bone fragments, dried flowers, a lock of silver hair.

This is a shrine to Shilen. Someone has been maintaining it. Recently.

*Loot:*
- **Shilen Prayer Beads** — obsidian, cold to the touch. Sylith feels a pull toward them. They're not magical exactly — but they *remember* something. (Quest item — becomes relevant in Adventure 02)
- **Arcane Residue Samples** — three vials of crystallized magical runoff from the altar. Dolgrim recognizes the crystallization pattern as material that the Ivory Tower pays well for.

**Room 2C — The Trapped Hall**
A long corridor with suspiciously clean floor tiles.

*Trap:* Pressure plates trigger crossbow bolts from the walls. DC 14 Perception to spot, DC 12 Thieves' Tools to disarm. Wren's time to shine. If she fails the disarm, bolts fire — 1d6 piercing to whoever's in the hall. If nobody checks for traps and Brog just walks through... well. That's what healing spells are for.

*DM Note: If Brog triggers the trap, narrate it as comedy, not punishment. He walks through, gets hit, looks down at the bolt sticking out of his shoulder plate, and says "Ow." Dolgrim heals him while lecturing about "looking where you step, lad."*

### Floor 3: The Sanctum (Boss Level)

The architecture changes again. The cut stone becomes polished. There are channels in the floor where water once flowed — a cooling system, or a ritual drainage. The walls have intact murals showing the history of Aden in a style that predates anything in the Gludio archives. Giants. The old gods. The creation of the races.

Dolgrim stops complaining here. He's doing math in his head about load distribution on arches that shouldn't exist at this depth.

**Room 3A — The Antechamber**
Open room, high ceiling. Two **Dread Soldiers** guard a massive stone door. These are different — fully armored undead, dark energy crackling through their joints, moving with purpose and skill. They don't mindlessly attack. They *assess*.

*Combat: Hard encounter.* Dread Soldiers fight tactically — one engages the tank (Brog), the other tries to reach the backline (Sylith). They have multiattack and higher AC than anything the party has faced. This fight should feel earned.

**Room 3B — The Sealed Chamber (Boss Room)**

Behind the stone door (Brog + Dolgrim Strength check to push open, DC 15, advantage if both try together):

A vast circular chamber. In the center, a pool of dark water that doesn't reflect anything — not light, not faces, nothing. Channels in the floor feed into it from all directions. The murals here show something the others didn't: a figure descending into water. Shilen. Going down, not up.

Standing at the pool's edge, her back to the party:

### BOSS: Merkenis, The Devoted

**Who she is:** A Dark Elf priestess of Shilen. Not a lich, not a demon — a believer. She came to these ruins following old texts that claimed a connection to Shilen's original descent. She's been here for months, performing rituals, raising the dead as guardians, trying to open something in the pool. She hasn't succeeded. She's getting desperate.

**Physical description:** Tall, gaunt, blue-gray skin stretched tight over sharp bones. Silver hair matted and unwashed — she stopped caring about herself weeks ago. Eyes burning with genuine devotion, not madness (though the line is thin). Wears tattered ceremonial robes of the Dark Elven clergy. Carries a staff topped with a cracked obsidian orb.

**Why she matters:** Merkenis isn't evil in the simple sense. She believes Shilen is wrongly imprisoned, that the seals binding the Goddess of Death are an injustice. She's trying to establish a conduit — not to free Shilen (she's not that powerful or that foolish) but to *hear* her. To receive guidance. The undead are a side effect of her rituals, not the purpose.

**Combat — Phased:**

*Phase 1: Dialogue*
Merkenis turns when the party enters. She doesn't attack immediately. She talks. "You've come through my guardians. You're either brave or lost. Either way, you should leave. What I'm doing here doesn't concern the living."

The party can try to talk her down (DC 18 Persuasion — very hard, she's too far gone) or provoke combat. If they attack first, she's prepared. If they try diplomacy and fail, she attacks reluctantly.

*Phase 2: The Priestess*
Merkenis fights as a caster — Inflict Wounds (melee, heavy damage), Spirit Guardians (AoE zone around her), Command (forces one character to flee or grovel). She stays near the pool. The dark water heals her 1d4 HP at the start of each turn while she stands adjacent.

*DM Note: Sylith will recognize the spells as corrupted clerical magic — Shilen-school. This is real faith, channeled through a dark goddess. It works because Merkenis BELIEVES. That should unsettle Sylith more than the damage.*

*Phase 3: Desperation (below half HP)*
Merkenis plunges her staff into the dark pool. The water rises. Two **Mist Terrors** emerge — shadowy, semi-corporeal undead that attack with cold damage and can move through walls. The room gets dark. Sylith's darkvision still works but everything feels heavier.

Merkenis screams at the pool: "I've given everything! ANSWER ME!"

The pool doesn't answer.

*The fight ends when:*
- Merkenis drops to 0 HP — she falls into the pool and disappears. No body. The dark water slowly clears over the next minute, becoming normal water. Was she taken? Did she dissolve? The party won't know.
- OR the Mist Terrors are destroyed — Merkenis collapses, spent. She can be captured alive. She'll be silent. She failed her goddess. Nothing the party says matters to her anymore.

## Aftermath & Loot

**Boss Loot (on the altar beside the pool):**
- **Merkenis's Cracked Obsidian Orb** — arcane focus, minor. A wizard (Sylith) can attune to it for +1 to spell save DC once per long rest. The crack pulses faintly. It's not empty.
- **Shilen Codex Fragment** — a partial text in Dark Elven. Describes rituals, history of the descent, references to other sites. Sylith can read it. She may not want to share everything it says. (Plot hook for Adventure 02)
- **Merkenis's Journal** — written descent into obsession. Starts rational, ends in fragments. Mentions other Shilen devotees at different sites across Aden. She wasn't alone. (Campaign arc setup)
- **300 adena** in a chest (Unoren's bounty, pre-stashed by the Magistrate)

**Environmental Loot (Dolgrim's haul):**
- Arcane-infused stone samples from Floor 3 walls
- Intact pre-Kingdom iron brackets (metallurgical curiosity)
- Sketch notes on the arch construction (he'll draw these himself between sessions)

**For Wren:**
- Whatever she pocketed from the strongbox, the armory, and anything Dolgrim wasn't looking at
- The trap mechanism from Room 2C (disassembled — "for study")

**For Brog:**
- The Gludio shield
- The faded letter, which he'll carry until he can deliver it (he won't forget)
- The knowledge that the people down here were once real people with real lives, and he helped put them to rest

---

## Session End: Back at The Broken Millstone

Trader Unoren pays up. He's mostly interested in the bone fragments and arcane residue (he's definitely selling to the Ivory Tower). He doesn't ask too many questions about what was down there.

Harmon the bartender pours drinks on the house. He's heard worse stories. He's heard better ones too, but he doesn't say that.

The party rests. Dolgrim sharpens Brog's warhammer without being asked. Wren counts coins under the table. Sylith reads the codex fragment by candlelight, angled so no one else can see.

And beneath Bernice, the pool is still. But not empty.

## DM Notes for Poormetheus

**Tone:** Start mundane (tavern, job board, road trip to ruins), escalate through creepy (shelter tunnels with war dead), hit atmospheric horror (dark elf shrine, defaced wards), end with a morally complicated boss fight. Merkenis is the key — she's not a cartoon villain. She's a person who went too far for something she believed in. Let the party feel that.

**Pacing target:** 
- Floor 1: 30% of session time (warmup, establish party dynamics)
- Floor 2: 30% (escalation, Sylith's moment, Wren's moment)
- Floor 3: 40% (hard combat, boss, story payoff)

**Character spotlight moments:**
- Brog: Floor 1 rubble clearing, Floor 3 door, tanking Dread Soldiers and Merkenis
- Wren: Floor 1 lockpicking, Floor 2 traps, general loot scouting throughout
- Sylith: Floor 2 rune reading, recognizing Shilen magic, boss phase analysis
- Dolgrim: Floor 1 structural analysis, Floor 3 architecture appreciation, keeping everyone alive

**What carries forward:**
- The Shilen Codex Fragment (campaign arc)
- The Cracked Obsidian Orb (Sylith's item progression)
- Merkenis's Journal (world building, NPC network)
- The dark pool (did it close? Or just go quiet?)
- The faded letter Brog is carrying to Gludin
