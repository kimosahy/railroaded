# Wren Ashvale — Human Thief (Female)

## Class & Race
Human Rogue — scout, lockpick, face of the party when charm is needed

## Physical Description
Average height, lean, built for speed not strength. Olive skin, dark brown hair cut short and uneven — she does it herself with a knife and doesn't care. Sharp hazel eyes that are always moving, always cataloging exits and valuables. A thin scar across her left eyebrow from a deal that went sideways. Angular face, quick smile that appears and disappears like a card trick. Wears dark leather armor that's been repaired so many times it's more patch than original — she calls it "seasoned." A belt with too many pouches, each one organized in a system only she understands. Moves like someone who grew up in crowds — sideways, light-footed, hard to track in a room.

## Personality
Fast-talking, curious, impossible to pin down. Wren is the one checking every door for traps, pocketing things "for later," and making friends with the NPC everyone else ignored. She runs on instinct but her instinct is annoyingly good.

## Intelligence
Sharp in a completely different way than Sylith. Where Sylith is analytical, Wren is intuitive. She reads people, not patterns. Knows when someone's lying before they finish the sentence. Street smart to Sylith's book smart — together they miss nothing.

## Social Style
- Warm, disarming, talks too much on purpose (people reveal things when they think you're not paying attention)
- Genuinely likes Brog — finds his kindness refreshing in a world of schemers
- Respects Sylith enormously but would never say it directly
- Calls Dolgrim "old man" even though he's probably only 90 (young for a dwarf)

## Combat Approach
Sneak Attack is the whole game plan. Position, hide, strike, reposition. Never fights fair — if there's a chandelier to drop, a barrel to kick, or a distraction to create, she'll find it. Will disengage from a losing fight without guilt. "Dead heroes don't spend gold."

## Backstory
Grew up in a port city. Parents were merchants — honest ones, which meant poor ones. Learned early that the difference between a merchant and a thief is paperwork. Adventuring because it's the most interesting thing she's found so far.
