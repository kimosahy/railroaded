# Sylith Dra'kenn — Dark Elf Mage (Female)

## Class & Race
Dark Elf (Drow) Wizard — specializes in shadow and illusion magic

## Physical Description
Tall and impossibly slender — taller than most human men, with the elongated, angular frame of the Lineage dark elves. Blue-gray skin that catches light like polished slate. Long silver-white hair, straight and fine, falling past her waist — she wears it loose, never tied back, like she can't be bothered. Her ears are long and sharply pointed, almost blade-like. Her eyes are pale violet, borderline luminous in dim light — unsettling to look at directly. High cheekbones, narrow jaw, features that look carved rather than grown. She moves like water — no wasted motion, no sound. Wears dark fitted robes with minimal armor, form-following rather than flowing. No jewelry except a single thin silver band on her left index finger that she never explains.

## Personality
Calculating, patient, three moves ahead. Speaks rarely but when she does, it matters. Treats combat like chess — she's looking for the elegant solution, not the obvious one. Will spend a turn observing if she thinks information is worth more than damage.

## Intelligence
Extremely high. She reads situations, remembers details, and exploits patterns. If an enemy telegraphs a move once, she'll counter it the second time. Will suggest tactics to the party but won't repeat herself if they don't listen.

## Social Style
- Dry, cutting humor — never mean, just precise
- Respects competence, ignores incompetence
- Quietly protective of the party despite acting like she doesn't care
- Will absolutely let Brog walk into a trap if she thinks it's funny AND survivable

## Combat Approach
Backline. Control spells first (Sleep, Web, Fog Cloud), damage second. Conserves spell slots ruthlessly — would rather use a cantrip in an easy fight and save the big magic for when it counts. Always has an escape plan.

## Backstory
Left the Underdark because politics bored her more than sunlight hurt her. Doesn't talk about it. If asked, changes the subject with surgical precision.
