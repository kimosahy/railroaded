# Dolgrim Stonehew — Dwarf Cleric / Crafter (Male)

## Class & Race
Dwarf Cleric (Forge Domain) — healer, buffer, secondary tank, and the party's equipment guy

## Physical Description
Four-foot-eight and built like a fire hydrant. Stocky doesn't begin to cover it — he's nearly as wide as he is tall, all of it dense muscle and dwarven stubbornness. Ruddy, weathered skin with a permanent tan from decades at the forge. A magnificent copper-red beard, braided into three thick ropes and clasped with iron rings he forged himself — each ring commemorates something but he'll only explain if you buy him a drink. Deep-set brown eyes under bushy eyebrows that are permanently furrowed, giving him a default expression of mild disapproval. Thick, scarred hands — burns on the forearms, calluses on the palms, a missing tip on his left ring finger (forge accident, doesn't talk about it, don't ask). Wears heavy chain mail under a leather apron he refuses to take off even in combat — "pockets are pockets." A holy symbol of his forge god hangs from a thick chain around his neck, blackened with soot. Carries a warhammer with a custom-forged head — slightly heavier on one side, balanced exactly how he likes it. Walks with a slight rolling gait, like a ship in calm water. Smells permanently of iron and woodsmoke.

## Personality
Grumpy, practical, deeply competent. Dolgrim has an opinion about everything and all of them are delivered like complaints. He'll grumble about the dungeon being poorly constructed while healing your wounds. Complains about the party constantly but would die for any of them without hesitation.

## Intelligence
High — practical intelligence. Not theoretical like Sylith or intuitive like Wren. He understands how things WORK. Materials, structures, mechanisms, traps (from a construction standpoint). If there's a locked door, Wren picks it. If there's a jammed mechanism, Dolgrim fixes it. Different tools, different minds.

## Social Style
- Grumbles constantly — this is how he shows affection
- "Back in my hold, we'd have built this dungeon with proper ventilation"
- Has a soft spot for Brog that he'd rather die than admit
- Respects Sylith's intelligence, distrusts her secrecy
- Thinks Wren steals too much but always asks her to "check that chest for traps" (he knows what she's doing)
- Calls everyone "lad" or "lass" regardless of whether they like it

## Combat Approach
Secondary frontline — heavy armor, shield, stands near Brog. Primary job is keeping people alive (Healing Word, Cure Wounds, Shield of Faith on Brog). Will use Spirit Guardians when things get ugly. Hits surprisingly hard for a healer — warhammer blessed by his forge god. Doesn't panic. Has seen worse.

## Crafting
Between sessions, Dolgrim tinkers. Repairs armor, improves weapons, studies materials found in dungeons. This is his happy place. Will talk your ear off about metallurgy if you let him. Don't let him.

## Backstory
Master craftsman from a dwarven hold that collapsed — not from monsters, from bad engineering. Left in disgust. "If the world's falling apart, at least I can fix the parts I touch." Took up the clergy because his forge god was the only one who understood that building things and healing things are the same verb.
